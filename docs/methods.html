3 Method

3.1 Data input

The terminology and analyses performed within this report are consistent with reports by (Biolink 2011; Gruber, Vysna, and Adamack 2014). Throughout the report the following terms are consistant:

  • Subareas are defined to capture a representative (and random) replicate across the entire study area (subareas’ = 5).

  • Each site is a single location in a subarea where the “absence” or “presence” of faecal pellets was collected. Sites represent unique locations between periods but some sites are sampled multiple times across all periods.

  • Period: over the duration of our study (2007-2020) there are several periods of differing sample design and data collection. This grouping allows us to account for these differences throughout the study.

  • Occurrence: is determined by the presence or absence of koala faecal pellets at a sampling site for a given period.

  • Active: a site or tree where at least one koala faecal pellet was found during a period.

  • Activity level:, which is the proportion of trees with one or more koala faecal pellets at sampling sites for every period.
